User Manual

Follow this guide to use all user-side features of ECCMS.

1. Set Up Your Profile

  1. Click Profile in the sidebar.
  2. Enter your First Name, Last Name, Middle Initial (optional), Birthdate, and Cellphone Number.
  3. Upload a profile picture if you want to personalize your account.
  4. Click Setup to save your profile information.

2. File a New Complaint

  1. Click File a Complaint in the sidebar.
  2. Select the complaint category. If you choose Others, enter a custom category.
  3. Enter the location and full description of your concern.
  4. Optionally attach an image using Take Photo (camera) or Choose from Files/Photos.
  5. Click Submit and keep the complaint code shown in the success message for tracking.

3. Track Complaint Status

  1. Click Status.
  2. Review all complaints you submitted, including complaint code, category, location, and date.
  3. Use Previous and Next to browse pages when you have many records.
  4. Monitor each complaint status such as Reported, Completed, Already in Work, and Reopened.

4. Reopen a Completed Complaint

  1. In Status, click the Complaint Code of a complaint with status Completed.
  2. A popup will appear asking for your reason.
  3. Enter why you want to reopen the complaint, then click Reopen Complaint.
  4. The complaint status will change to Reopened and it will be sent back to admin for review.

5. View Notification Logs

  1. Click Notifications.
  2. Check the notification badge in the sidebar to see if you have new updates.
  3. Review updates sent when an admin changes your complaint status.
  4. For Reported or Deleted notifications, click the notification row to view the admin's reason.
  5. Check details such as complaint code, action type, message, and date.

6. Update Profile Any Time

  1. Go back to Profile.
  2. Edit details or change your profile picture as needed.
  3. Click Setup again to save updates.

7. Read Help, About, and Contact

  1. Click Help to open this user guide any time.
  2. Click About us to view the system background and development team information.
  3. Click Contact Us to view the support phone number and email.

8. Log Out Securely

  1. Click Logout when you are done.
  2. Always log out on shared or public devices.

About Us

The Electronic City Complaint Management System (ECCMS) is a web-based platform developed to improve the efficiency and organization of handling community complaints and concerns. In many communities, reporting issues such as public disturbances, damaged infrastructure, sanitation problems, and other civic concerns can be difficult due to slow manual processes, lack of proper documentation, and limited communication between residents and local administrators. The ECCMS was designed to address these challenges by providing a centralized and accessible digital system that simplifies the process of submitting, recording, and managing complaints.

The primary purpose of the Electronic City Complaint Management System is to create a structured and reliable platform where citizens can easily report issues affecting their community. Through this system, users are able to submit complaints electronically by providing necessary details such as the category of the complaint, location, and description of the issue. Once submitted, the complaint is securely stored in the system database and can be reviewed by authorized administrators through a dedicated administrative dashboard. This organized approach allows administrators to monitor complaints, evaluate reports, and manage responses more effectively.

In addition to simplifying the complaint submission process, the system promotes transparency and accountability in the handling of community concerns. By digitizing the reporting and tracking process, the ECCMS ensures that complaints are properly documented and accessible for review. This reduces the risk of complaints being lost, ignored, or improperly handled. The system also helps administrators maintain records of reported issues, which can assist in identifying recurring problems within the community and developing more effective solutions.

The Electronic City Complaint Management System was also developed with data privacy and security in mind. User information and complaint records are handled responsibly and stored within a controlled environment to protect the confidentiality of individuals who submit reports. The system is designed to support responsible communication between residents and administrators while maintaining the integrity of the information collected.

Furthermore, the development of this system aims to demonstrate the potential of information technology in improving public service and community management. By integrating modern web technologies and database systems, the ECCMS showcases how digital platforms can support more efficient governance and better interaction between citizens and authorities.
